League setup

  • Game nights: Tuesdays at 7:00, 8:15, and 9:30 PM.
  • Game format: three 17‑minute running‑time periods with a 3‑minute warm‑up and 1‑minute breaks.
  • On‑ice: 5 skaters per team, pond hockey goals, no goalies.
  • Referees are provided as part of league fees.
  • League size: 8 teams, 14 games plus playoffs.

Rosters, drafting & new players

This is a strictly non‑competitive recreational co‑ed league with players of all abilities. Play to have fun, mentor teammates, and keep things safe and friendly. Captains and referees will help uphold this standard.

  • All players must be registered on an official roster and must sign the waiver before playing. Rosters freeze for playoffs on February 1.
  • New players may be allocated to teams at the commissioner’s discretion, including skaters joining mid‑season. Players may play on only one team.
  • If a team is short skaters for a game, they may add subs registered on another Tuesday team, not to exceed 10 total players. Subs must be agreed to before the game with the opposing captain.
  • Roster violations result in forfeit of affected games.

Gameplay rules

Equipment

  • Required: helmet, shin pads, and hockey skates.
  • Other protective gear is optional but strongly recommended.

No check

Body checking is not allowed. Examples include:

  • Intentional physical contact with no attempt to play the puck.
  • Using hip, shoulder, or forearm contact to force an opponent off the puck.
  • Impediment of progress with the body without attempting to gain possession.
Penalty: Minor penalty for body contact in these instances.

Shots & passes

  • No slapshots; doing so results in a minor penalty.
  • Saucer passes are allowed if kept below the waist. Passes above the waist result in a loss of possession.
  • Shots must originate from inside the blue line and outside the crease. Goals scored outside these bounds do not count and result in a loss of possession.

Face‑offs & change of possession

  • Face‑offs: start of each period, after penalties, and after each goal.
  • Possession is awarded to the defending team after offsides or icing.
  • If the puck goes out of play, the team that last touched it loses possession.
  • On changes of possession, teams must allow opponents ½‑ice (to the red line) to advance.
  • The team with possession should allow a brief setup before advancing. Advancing too soon or stalling can result in a loss of possession at the referee’s discretion.

Goaltending (“242” rule)

No camping in the crease. Skaters may pass through or play the puck in the crease, but no one may remain in the crease while the puck is in play for more than 2 seconds.

Penalty: 2‑minute minor to the offending player.

Penalties

Except where noted above, the league follows USA Hockey rules. We use a progressive in‑game penalty system:

  • First minor: 2 minutes
  • Second minor: 5 minutes
  • Third minor: ejection plus a 5‑minute bench penalty

Ringer rule

No player may score more than three (3) goals in a game. A fourth goal does not count and results in a loss of possession.

Zero tolerance

Hockey is competitive, but we expect everyone to play within their ability and with respect for others on the ice. Referees will call unsportsmanlike conduct for inappropriate behavior.

  • No threatening language or physical altercations.
  • No arguing with officials; talking back, especially in an offensive manner, is grounds for ejection.
  • Repeat offenders may forfeit rink privileges.

Captains are responsible for setting the tone and helping maintain a safe, enjoyable environment.
